Privacy notice

What happens to the information you send when you ask about a page audit or work with TradeSite Sprint.

Last updated: 10 July 2026

At a glance: this website has no custom analytics, advertising trackers, cookies or server-side enquiry form. The audit form opens a draft in your own email app.

1. Who is responsible for your information?

TradeSite Sprint is the service name used on this website by an independent website provider. It is not presented as a limited company. The provider is the controller of personal information sent in connection with an audit request or booking. You can contact the provider at adnanmunye@googlemail.com. The provider's legal identity and business contact details will also appear on booking and invoice documents.

2. Information that may be handled

If you contact or book the service, this may include:

  • your name, role, business name and contact details;
  • your website URL, project brief, messages and feedback;
  • brand assets, account-access details or business facts you choose to provide; and
  • booking, payment and invoice records. Full card details are not requested by this website.

3. How the information is obtained

Information is received directly from you by email and, if a project goes ahead, through the channels agreed for delivery. The form on the main page does not transmit data to this website: it prepares an email for you to review and send using your device.

The site is designed for static hosting. The hosting provider may create basic technical logs as part of serving the site; those logs are governed by that provider's own notices and are not used here for advertising or visitor profiling.

4. Business outreach

TradeSite Sprint may carry out narrowly targeted B2B outreach to UK companies that appear relevant to this service. Research may use a company's public name and website, a public role-based inbox, public Companies House status, and high-level technical observations from the company's public website. It does not use purchased contact lists or tracking pixels.

The lawful basis is legitimate interests in introducing a relevant business service while keeping the approach limited and proportionate. An initial message may be followed up once if there is no reply. You can opt out by replying to any message; the request will be honoured. Research about a business that does not respond is deleted or minimised after 90 days. A minimal suppression record may be kept so an objection is not accidentally ignored later.

5. Why other information is used

  • To review and reply to an audit or project enquiry.
  • To prepare a scope, take steps towards a contract and deliver agreed work.
  • To maintain security, project and financial records.
  • To meet applicable tax, accounting or legal duties.

The usual lawful bases are taking steps at your request before a contract, performing a contract, legitimate interests in operating and protecting a small B2B service, and complying with legal obligations. Information is not sold, and there is no automated decision-making.

6. Who it may be shared with

Information may be handled by essential service providers, such as email, hosting, file-transfer, invoicing or payment providers, only where needed to respond or deliver the service. It may also be disclosed where required by law. If a supplier processes information outside the UK, appropriate provider safeguards will be relied on where required.

7. How long it is kept

Enquiry and project information is kept only as long as reasonably needed for the conversation, delivery, security and follow-up. It is then deleted or minimised unless invoice, tax, dispute or other legal records need to be retained for longer. Access credentials should be shared only when needed and are removed from working records after the relevant access is no longer required.

8. Your choices and rights

Depending on the circumstances, UK data-protection law may give you rights to access, correct, erase, restrict or object to the use of your personal information, and to receive a portable copy. You can ask a question or exercise a right by emailing the address above. Identity may need to be checked before disclosing information.

You can also raise a concern with the Information Commissioner's Office. Please consider contacting TradeSite Sprint first so the concern can be addressed directly.

9. Changes to this notice

This notice may be updated if the website or working process changes. The date at the top shows the latest version.
