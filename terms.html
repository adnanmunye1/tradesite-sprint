Service terms

The practical ground rules for the fixed-price TradeSite Sprint. A written booking summary will confirm the project-specific details.

Last updated: 10 July 2026

Important: the sprint is offered business-to-business, not to consumers. These website terms are a concise starting point; the written scope and booking confirmation take priority for a particular project.

1. The service provider

TradeSite Sprint is the service name used by an independent website provider and is not presented as a limited company. The provider's legal identity, contact details and payment details will be given in the written booking or invoice before payment is requested.

2. Who can book

The offer is intended for UK limited-company trades and local-service firms acting for business purposes. The person booking confirms that they are authorised to approve the work and supply the company's content, assets and access.

3. Standard sprint scope

Unless the written booking says otherwise, the £400 fixed-price sprint includes:

  • one homepage or landing page of up to six sections;
  • light editing and restructuring of existing copy and calls to action;
  • a responsive front-end build;
  • agreed click-to-call, WhatsApp or enquiry routes using client-supplied details;
  • basic metadata and LocalBusiness schema using verified client-supplied facts;
  • optimisation of suitable supplied images;
  • before-and-after Lighthouse reports; and
  • one consolidated revision round.

Ecommerce, full branding, paid plugins, photography, multi-page copywriting, custom applications and ongoing marketing are not included. Additional work needs a separate written scope and price.

4. Booking and payment

A booking is confirmed when the written scope is accepted and the first £200 payment has cleared. The remaining £200 is due after staging approval and before the final build is published, transferred or made live. Prices are stated in pounds sterling. Any applicable tax treatment will be shown on the invoice.

5. The 48-hour timing

The 48-hour period covers delivery of the first staging build. It starts only when the booking payment, agreed brief, usable content and assets, verified business facts and any essential access have all been received. Waiting for client material, clarification, feedback, third-party services or new requirements pauses or moves the timetable. Revision and launch happen after the client returns one consolidated set of feedback.

6. Client responsibilities

The client is responsible for:

  • supplying accurate, lawful and up-to-date business information;
  • having the right to use all names, logos, copy, photographs and other material supplied;
  • reviewing spelling, prices, claims, accreditations, service areas, contact routes, policies and structured data on staging;
  • providing feedback and approvals promptly through an authorised contact; and
  • maintaining secure hosting, domain and account access after handover unless separately agreed.

7. Revisions and approval

One revision means one consolidated list of reasonable changes within the agreed scope. New sections, a new direction, replacement source material or piecemeal rounds are additional work. Approval can be given by email. If factual corrections are needed before approval, they should be identified in the revision list.

8. Cancellation and delay

The first payment reserves time and covers setup and work begun. If the client cancels after work starts, the provider may retain the reasonable amount relating to work already done and committed time, and will explain the calculation. If the provider cancels without delivering the paid-for work, the undelivered portion will be refunded. If required client input is not received for 30 days, the project may be closed and any restart may need a new timetable or scope.

9. Ownership and licences

The client keeps ownership of material it supplies. Once the project is paid in full, the client may use the final page created for its business. The provider keeps ownership of pre-existing tools, know-how and reusable code patterns, while granting the client the permission reasonably needed to use them as part of the final page. Open-source software, fonts, platforms and other third-party materials remain subject to their own licences and terms.

10. Performance and third parties

Lighthouse reports are point-in-time technical measurements and scores can change with hosting, content, devices or tool updates. The provider does not guarantee search ranking, traffic, leads, sales, conversion rate, uninterrupted hosting or third-party service availability. No work outside the written scope is implied.

11. Responsibility if something goes wrong

Each party will take reasonable steps to limit avoidable loss. To the extent permitted by law, the provider is not responsible for indirect or consequential business loss, loss caused by inaccurate or unlicensed client material, client or third-party changes, or services outside the provider's control. Any overall liability connected with a sprint is limited to the fees paid for that sprint. Nothing in these terms limits liability where the law does not allow it to be limited, including for fraud or death or personal injury caused by negligence.

12. Confidentiality and access

Non-public information supplied for the project will be used only to assess, deliver or administer the work, unless disclosure is required by law. Both parties should use secure sharing methods and grant only the minimum access needed. Access can be removed after launch or handover.
